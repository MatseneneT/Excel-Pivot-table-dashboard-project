# Excel-Pivot-table-dashboard-project
I developed a comprehensive dashboard in Excel to analyze data. This process included data cleaning, data visualization by transforming raw customer data into an interactive bike sales dashboard in Excel using Pivot tables to assist with Business decision making.
## Data set used
-<a href="https://github.com/AlexTheAnalyst/Excel-Tutorial/blob/main/Excel%20Project%20Dataset.xlsx">Data Set</a>

## Objectives:
-How does average income affect a customers decision to buy a bike?
- Does commute distance influence bike purchases?
- Which age group is most likely to buy a bike?

## Key steps
Step 1: Data Cleaning and Preparation
- Remove Duplicates, Create Age Brackets using IF statement, write names if full to avoid confusion, etc. 
Step 2: Pivot Tables and Visualisations
Three key pivot tables are created, each supporting a different visualisation:
- Income by Gender, Commute Distance, Age Brackets
Step 3: Dashboard Assembly
The final step is to combine the charts into a professional-looking dashboard. And inserting Slicers to make a dashboard interactive.
- 
## Key insights:
- Average income affects positively a customers decision to buy a bike. Customer's with higher income are more likely to buy a bike
- The highest number of purchases comes from customers with a 5-10 minute commute, indicating the bikes are often used for practical travel
- The middle aged group (31-54 years old) are most likely to buy a bike. representing the company largest and most valuable data base

## Recommendations and conclusion
Recommendations
- Direct advertising budgets toward the 31–54 age group, emphasizing high-performance models that align with their higher disposable income.
- Bundle bikes with accessories like mudguards and panniers to appeal to the high-volume 5–10 mile commute segment.
- Offer premium tiers for high-income earners and entry-level financing options to capture potential growth in lower-income brackets.
- Concentrate sales efforts in regions where residential areas are located within a 10-mile radius of major employment centers.

Conclusion
This project successfully converted raw data into actionable insights, revealing that bike purchases are driven by financial capacity, middle-age life stages, and moderate commute distances. By aligning product offerings and marketing strategies with these specific customer profiles, the business can optimize its resources and significantly increase conversion rates.
